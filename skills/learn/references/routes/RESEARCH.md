# Research route

Use when trustworthy knowledge must be established before teaching or when a claim is current, contested, safety-relevant, or version-specific.

## Sequence

1. Define the research question, scope, and decision it informs.
2. Search primary research, official documentation, standards, and authoritative practitioners.
3. Group findings by claim or disagreement, not by source order.
4. Distinguish verified findings, interpretation, disagreement, risk, and open gaps.
5. Update the mission-level `RESOURCES.md` with annotated sources that earned continued use.
6. Produce a cited Markdown or HTML brief only when a durable synthesis will help the mission.
7. Translate findings into implications for the capability map or next lesson without outrunning the evidence.

Research is preparation, not proof that the learner acquired a capability.
